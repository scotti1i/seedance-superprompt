# Contributing

## Status — solo-maintained

> **This repo is currently maintained solo by
> [@scotti1i](https://github.com/scotti1i).**
> **Pull requests are not being accepted at this stage.**

Why: I want the methodology, lint rules, templates, and examples to stay
internally consistent while the project is still finding its voice. Once
the structure is stable, I'll reopen PRs and grow a contributor base
with proper review guidelines.

## What you can still do

Everything here is **MIT-licensed**, so you can:

- ⭐ **Star** the repo to follow updates
- 🍴 **Fork** it freely for personal or commercial use
- 🛠️ **Modify** anything in your own fork
- 📤 **Redistribute** with attribution (per MIT license)

## How to help anyway

The most valuable thing you can give right now: **issues**.

### Open an issue for any of these:

1. **Bug** — the skill produced wrong output (use the bug template)
2. **Lint rule proposal** — you've spotted an anti-pattern not yet covered
   (use the lint-rule template; include your test evidence)
3. **Template proposal** — you've engineered a great template and want to
   share the structure (use the new-template template; I may write the
   verified version)
4. **Methodology disagreement** — you think a rule in `methodology.md` is
   wrong or outdated; bring sources
5. **Question** — you tried the skill and something didn't work as
   documented

Issue templates are at [`.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/`](.github/ISSUE_TEMPLATE/).

### What I will do with your issue

- Acknowledge within ~7 days
- Discuss the design / evidence
- If accepted, I incorporate it into the codebase myself with attribution
  (`@your-handle`) in the commit message and CHANGELOG

## When will PRs reopen?

When at least one of these is true:

- The repo has 50+ stars (a signal there's real demand)
- A new template / rule category needs more than one author's
  perspective
- I find a long-term co-maintainer

Until then — issues over PRs, always.

## Code of conduct

Be specific, cite sources where possible, no flame wars. I'll close
issues that don't follow the templates or that read as low-effort.
